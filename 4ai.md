# Glutt Landing Page Brief (for AI agent)

Use this as source-of-truth context when building the marketing site for **Glutt**.

## 1) What Glutt is

Glutt is an iOS cooking app that helps people go from "I found food content online" to "I actually cooked tonight" with less friction.

Core promise:
- Save recipes from links/social/screenshot
- Plan meals around real life
- Use what you already have
- Cook with confidence
- Waste less food

This is not a social feed app. It is a practical cooking + planning assistant.

## 2) Who it's for

Primary users:
- Busy people who save recipe content but rarely cook it
- Home cooks who want less mental load (planning, groceries, pantry decisions)
- Fitness-conscious users who care about protein/calorie awareness, but don't want a complicated tracker

User pain points:
- "I save recipes everywhere and lose them"
- "I don't know what to cook with what I already have"
- "Meal planning is annoying"
- "I buy groceries, then still order food"
- "Leftovers go bad"

## 3) Product pillars to communicate

1. **Save from anywhere**
   - Import from TikTok/Instagram/YouTube/web links
   - Screenshot import
   - Share-sheet flow

2. **Cook from your real kitchen**
   - Pantry inventory
   - Missing-ingredient checks
   - "Use what I have" optimization
   - Invent a dish from pantry ingredients (AI)

3. **Plan + execute**
   - Meal planning
   - Grocery list generation
   - Cook mode with guided steps/timers

4. **Smart, but practical AI**
   - Recipe cleanup for messy imports
   - Recipe adjustment (higher protein/lighter/cheaper/food rules)
   - Leftover remix ideas
   - Pantry/meal photo analysis

5. **Close the loop**
   - Leftovers tracking
   - Food logging (manual + photo estimate)
   - Progress view

## 4) Tone + brand direction

Voice:
- Calm, confident, practical
- Friendly but not gimmicky
- "Kitchen sidekick," not "AI robot"

Avoid:
- Hype words like "revolutionary" / "world-class"
- Overly technical jargon
- Looking like a data-heavy dashboard

Visual direction:
- Warm, clean, modern
- Food-first visuals
- Simple hierarchy and obvious CTAs

## 5) Landing page goals

Primary goal:
- Get users to trust the product quickly and want to download/join waitlist.

Secondary goals:
- Explain what makes Glutt different in under 10 seconds.
- Show that it handles real-life cooking chaos (imports, pantry constraints, time pressure).
- Build credibility for App Store approval and real product maturity.

## 6) Suggested page structure

1. **Hero**
   - Headline: one-line value prop
   - Subhead: practical benefit
   - Primary CTA: "Get Glutt" or "Join the waitlist"
   - Secondary CTA: "See how it works"

2. **Problem -> outcome strip**
   - "Save recipes everywhere" -> "Cook more, waste less"

3. **Feature blocks (top 4)**
   - Save from anywhere
   - Cook from what you have
   - Plan + grocery + cook mode
   - AI that actually helps

4. **How it works (3 steps)**
   - Import
   - Plan
   - Cook

5. **Proof section**
   - Product screenshots / realistic UI mockups
   - Optional: "Built for real home cooking, not content scrolling"

6. **Privacy/trust section**
   - Local-first mindset + clear privacy policy/support links
   - "You control your data"

7. **Final CTA**
   - Repeat download/waitlist CTA

8. **Footer**
   - Privacy Policy
   - Terms
   - Support
   - Contact

## 7) Messaging examples (can adapt)

Headline options:
- "From saved recipes to actual dinner."
- "Cook what you save."
- "Your kitchen, finally organized."

Subhead options:
- "Import recipes from anywhere, plan your week, and cook with what you already have."
- "Glutt turns links, screenshots, and pantry ingredients into meals you'll actually make."

CTA options:
- "Get Glutt"
- "Join the waitlist"
- "See it in action"

## 8) Important constraints

- Keep copy concrete and benefit-driven.
- Show practical outcomes, not just feature names.
- Prioritize mobile-first design.
- Do not imply features we don't ship.
- Keep legal/support links visible in footer.

## 9) Current status context

- iOS app exists and is actively being prepared for full App Store launch.
- Core features are implemented (import, planning, pantry, AI helpers, cook flow).
- Accounts + cloud sync are being moved into required launch scope.
